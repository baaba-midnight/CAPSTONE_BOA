# Interview Questions: Evaluating the Adaptive AI IDE

Purpose:
    To understand students’ experiences and perceptions of the Adaptive AI IDE, particularly how it supports learning, provides feedback, and influences their programming approach.

## Section 1: Introduction

1. Can you briefly describe your experience using the Adaptive AI IDE?
2. How often did you use the system during your coursework?

## Section 2: Expectations and First Impressions

3. Before using the system, what were your expectations about how the AI would help you?
4. Did the system meet, exceed, or fall short of your expectations? Please explain why.

## Section 3: Learning Support and Scaffolding

5. Did the system’s feedback or suggestions help you understand your mistakes, or did it mainly give you the correct answers?
6. Would you say the system teaches you how to solve problems, or does it mostly spoon-feed the solution?
7. In what ways did the AI’s responses make it easier or harder for you to learn programming concepts?
8. Did the system provide hints or guidance that encouraged you to think through problems yourself (scaffolding)? Can you give an example?
9. How did using the system affect your confidence in solving coding problems on your own?

## Section 4: Usability and Experience

10.	Was the system easy to use and understand? Why or why not?
11.	Did you encounter any challenges, errors, or frustrations while using it?
12.	How did you feel about the balance between human effort and AI assistance when coding?

## Section 5: Overall Impact and Improvement

13.	Do you feel the Adaptive AI IDE helped you learn programming more effectively? Why or why not?
14.	What aspects of the system did you find most helpful?
15.	What would you like to see improved in the system’s design or AI behaviour?
16.	Would you recommend this system for other programming students? Why or why not?

## Section 6: Wrap-Up

17.	Is there anything else you’d like to share about your experience using the Adaptive AI IDE?
